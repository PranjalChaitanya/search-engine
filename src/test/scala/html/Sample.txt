<p><b>Databricks, Inc.</b> is an American software company based in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> It was founded in 2013 by the original creators of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apache_Spark" title="Apache Spark">Apache Spark</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_1-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-Reuters-1"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> It offers a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_computing" title="Cloud computing">cloud-based platform</a> for <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_analytics" class="mw-redirect" title="Data analytics">data analytics</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Databricks developed the '<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_lakehouse" class="mw-redirect" title="Data lakehouse">data lakehouse</a>' architecture, which combines elements of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_warehouse" title="Data warehouse">data warehouses</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_lake" title="Data lake">data lakes</a> for managing structured and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unstructured_data" title="Unstructured data">unstructured data</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The company develops <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delta_Lake_(Software)" class="mw-redirect" title="Delta Lake (Software)">Delta Lake</a>, an open-source project that adds ACID transaction support to data lakes.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Databricks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2013–2021"><span id="2013.E2.80.932021"></span>2013–2021</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Databricks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2013–2021"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1034237262">.mw-parser-output .stack{box-sizing:border-box}.mw-parser-output .stack>div{margin:1px;overflow:hidden}@media all and (min-width:720px){.mw-parser-output .stack-clear-left{float:left;clear:left}.mw-parser-output .stack-clear-right{float:right;clear:right}.mw-parser-output .stack-left{float:left}.mw-parser-output .stack-right{float:right}.mw-parser-output .stack-margin-clear-left{float:left;clear:left;margin-right:1em}.mw-parser-output .stack-margin-clear-right{float:right;clear:right;margin-left:1em}.mw-parser-output .stack-margin-left{float:left;margin-right:1em}.mw-parser-output .stack-margin-right{float:right;margin-left:1em}}</style><div class="stack mw-stack stack-right"><div><figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:DatabricksBooth.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="./Databricks - Wikipedia_files/DatabricksBooth.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="187" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/DatabricksBooth.jpg/500px-DatabricksBooth.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="2992"></a><figcaption>Databricks booth (2023)</figcaption></figure></div></div>
<p>Databricks grew out of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AMPLab" title="AMPLab">AMPLab</a> project at <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a> that was involved in making <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apache_Spark" title="Apache Spark">Apache Spark</a>, an <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source" class="mw-redirect" title="Open-source">open-source</a> distributed computing framework built atop <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scala_(programming_language)" title="Scala (programming language)">Scala</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The company was founded by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ali_Ghodsi" title="Ali Ghodsi">Ali Ghodsi</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andy_Konwinski" title="Andy Konwinski">Andy Konwinski</a>, Arsalan Tavakoli-Shiraji, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ion_Stoica" title="Ion Stoica">Ion Stoica</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matei_Zaharia" title="Matei Zaharia">Matei Zaharia</a>, Patrick Wendell, and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reynold_Xin" title="Reynold Xin">Reynold Xin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Microsoft Azure integrated Databricks as Azure Databricks in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2021, together with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Cloud" class="mw-redirect" title="Google Cloud">Google Cloud</a>, Databricks provided integration with the Google <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kubernetes" title="Kubernetes">Kubernetes</a> Engine and Google's <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BigQuery" title="BigQuery">BigQuery</a> platform.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In February 2021, the company reported having more than 5,000 customers.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_13-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-:0-13"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2022–present"><span id="2022.E2.80.93present"></span>2022–present</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Databricks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2022–present"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Databricks announced the Data Intelligence Platform, integrating its lakehouse architecture with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_AI" class="mw-redirect" title="Generative AI">generative AI</a> capabilities acquired from MosaicML.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The firm was valued at $62 billion in December 2024,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-:1-15"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> following a $10 billion funding round.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In early March 2025, Databricks announced it would invest $1 billion in San Francisco's downtown.<sup id="cite_ref-LauraWaxmann_2025_SFChornicle_17-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-LauraWaxmann_2025_SFChornicle-17"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2025, Databricks entered a five-year partnership with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic" title="Anthropic">Anthropic</a>, incorporating Anthropic's AI products into its platform in a deal valued at $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-HinduBreaur_2025_TheHindu_18-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-HinduBreaur_2025_TheHindu-18"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BelleLin_2025_WallStreetJournal_19-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-BelleLin_2025_WallStreetJournal-19"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> 
</p><p>In June 2025, Databricks entered into a four-year partnership with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphabet_Inc." title="Alphabet Inc.">Alphabet</a> to incorporate <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemini_(language_model)" title="Gemini (language model)">Gemini</a> into the Databricks platform.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The company launched Agent Bricks,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> a suite of tools to help organizations build AI agents, and Lakebase,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> an <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Online_transaction_processing" title="Online transaction processing">OLTP</a> database. 
</p><p>In April 2025, Databricks was featured in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> AI 50 list.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> 
</p><p>In September 2025, Databricks entered into a partnership with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenAI" title="OpenAI">OpenAI</a> to incorporate the company's LLMs into the Databricks platform in a deal valued at $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Databricks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acquisitions"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In June 2020, Databricks bought Redash, an open-source tool for data visualization and building of interactive dashboards.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In 2021, it bought German <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No-code_development_platform" title="No-code development platform">no-code</a> company 8080 Labs whose product, bamboolib, allowed data exploration without any coding.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In May 2023, Databricks bought data security group Okera, extending Databricks data governance capabilities.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In June, it bought the open-source generative AI startup MosaicML for $1.4<span class="nowrap">&nbsp;</span>billion.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In October, Databricks bought data replication startup Arcion for $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In 2024, Databricks bought data-management startup Tabular for over $1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2023, in response to the popularity of OpenAI's <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT" title="ChatGPT">ChatGPT</a>, the company introduced an open-source <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_model" title="Language model">language model</a>, named Dolly after <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dolly_(sheep)" title="Dolly (sheep)">Dolly the sheep</a>, that developers could use to create custom <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chatbot" title="Chatbot">chatbots</a>. Dolly has only 6 billion parameters.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Databricks claimed that Dolly had "ChatGPT-like instruction following ability", but has not released formal <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benchmark_(computing)" title="Benchmark (computing)">benchmark</a> tests comparing it to ChatGPT.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Databricks reported $1.6 billion in revenue for the 2023 fiscal year, demonstrating growth from the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 2025, Databricks acquired a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serverless_computing" title="Serverless computing">serverless</a> database startup, Neon,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> for around $1 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Funding">Funding</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Databricks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Funding"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In September 2013, Databricks announced it raised $13.9 million from <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andreessen_Horowitz" title="Andreessen Horowitz">Andreessen Horowitz</a> and said it aimed to offer an alternative to Google's <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MapReduce" title="MapReduce">MapReduce</a> system.<sup id="cite_ref-gigaom-series-a_39-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-gigaom-series-a-39"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> was a noted investor of Databricks in 2019, participating in the company's Series E at an unspecified amount.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The company has raised $1.9 billion in funding, including a $1 billion Series G led by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franklin_Templeton_Investments" title="Franklin Templeton Investments">Franklin Templeton</a> at a $28 billion post-money valuation in February 2021. Other investors include <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services" title="Amazon Web Services">Amazon Web Services</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CapitalG" title="CapitalG">CapitalG</a> (a growth equity firm under <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphabet_Inc." title="Alphabet Inc.">Alphabet Inc.</a>) and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salesforce_Ventures" class="mw-redirect" title="Salesforce Ventures">Salesforce Ventures</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_13-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-:0-13"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In August 2021, Databricks finished its eighth round of funding by raising $1.6 billion and valuing the company at $38 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-43"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
In December 2024, Databricks announced a $10 billion financing at a valuation of $62 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-:1-15"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In August 2025, Databricks announced a $1 billion Series K funding round, raising their valuation to over $100 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_44-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-:2-44"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Just 3 months later, in December 2025, Databricks completed a $4 billion Series L funding round at a new valuation of $134 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-45"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Products">Products</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Databricks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Products"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Databricks develops a cloud data platform referred to as a 'lakehouse', combining features of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_warehouse" title="Data warehouse">data warehouses</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_lake" title="Data lake">data lakes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-56"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>56<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>  The platform is built on the open-source Apache Spark framework, enabling analytical queries on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-structured_data" title="Semi-structured data">semi-structured data</a> without requiring a traditional <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database_schema" title="Database schema">database schema</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-57"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>57<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In October 2022, Lakehouse received <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FedRAMP" title="FedRAMP">FedRAMP</a> authorization for use with the U.S. federal government and contractors.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-58"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>58<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The company has also created Delta Lake, MLflow and Koalas, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_source" title="Open source">open source</a> projects that span <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_engineering" title="Data engineering">data engineering</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_science" title="Data science">data science</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning" title="Machine learning">machine learning</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-59"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>59<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-60"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>60<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2020, Databricks launched Delta Engine, a fast query engine for Delta Lake,<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-61"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>61<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> compatible with Apache Spark and MLflow.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-62"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>62<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2020, Databricks introduced Databricks SQL (previously called SQL Analytics) for running <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_intelligence" title="Business intelligence">business intelligence</a> and analytics reporting on top of data lakes. Analysts can query data sets with standard SQL or use connectors to integrate with business intelligence tools like Holistics, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tableau_Software" title="Tableau Software">Tableau</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qlik" title="Qlik">Qlik</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigma_(analytics_company)" title="Sigma (analytics company)">Sigma</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Looker_(company)" title="Looker (company)">Looker</a>, and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ThoughtSpot" title="ThoughtSpot">ThoughtSpot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-63"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>63<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Databricks offers a platform for other workloads, including machine learning, data storage and processing, streaming analytics, and business intelligence.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-64"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>64<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2024, Databricks released the Mosaic set of tools for customizing, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fine-tuning_(deep_learning)" title="Fine-tuning (deep learning)">fine-tuning</a> and building AI systems. It includes AI Vector Search for building <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retrieval-augmented_generation" title="Retrieval-augmented generation">RAG</a> models; AI Model Serving, a service for deploying, governing, querying and monitoring models fine-tuned or pre-deployed by Databricks; and AI Pretraining, a platform for enterprises to create their own LLMs.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-65"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>65<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2024, Databricks released its <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DBRX" title="DBRX">DBRX</a> foundation model under the Databricks Open Model License.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-66"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>66<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> It has a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mixture_of_experts" title="Mixture of experts">mixture-of-experts</a> architecture and is built on the MegaBlocks open-source project.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-67"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>67<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> DBRX cost $10 million to create. According to the company, DBRX performed competitively on industry benchmarks against other open-source models available at the time.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2025)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It beat other models like Llama 2 at solving logic puzzles and answering general knowledge questions, among other tasks. While it has 136 billion parameters, it only uses 36 billion, on average, to generate outputs.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-68"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>68<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> According to Databricks, DBRX can be used as a foundation for companies to build customized AI models using their proprietary data.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-69"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>69<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary,_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The whole paragraph is currently based on sources from Databricks, despite containing promotional claims about performance (January 2026)">non-primary source needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In addition to building the Databricks platform, the company has co-organized <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mooc" class="mw-redirect" title="Mooc">massive open online courses</a> about Spark<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-70"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>70<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and a conference for the Spark community called the Data + AI Summit,<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-71"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>71<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> formerly known as Spark Summit.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-72"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>72<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research#Primary,_secondary_and_tertiary_sources" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable secondary sources. (January 2026)">non-primary source needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>At the 2025 Data + AI Summit, Databricks introduced Agent Bricks, a development platform for AI agents,<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-73"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>73<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Lakebase, a transactional database,<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-74"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>74<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and Databricks One, a no-code AI business intelligence platform.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-75"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>75<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The company also disclosed its Databricks SQL product would grow to a $1 billion revenue run rate.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-76"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>76<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Collaborations">Collaborations</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Databricks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Collaborations"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In December 2024, Databricks along with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiz,_Inc." title="Wiz, Inc.">Wiz</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Workday,_Inc." title="Workday, Inc.">Workday</a> has decided to run their products on top of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services" title="Amazon Web Services">AWS</a> via the new button called "Buy with AWS".<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-77"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>77<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2025, Databricks announced a partnership with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Cloud_Platform" title="Google Cloud Platform">Google Cloud</a> to integrate its platform with Google Cloud services.<sup id="cite_ref-DatabricksGoogleCloud2_78-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Databricks#cite_note-DatabricksGoogleCloud2-78"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>78<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
